Lycanthrobia

Game Concept

The game takes place in a forest in the nighttime. Your character is a hunter and a skilled tracker who was out in the forest and attacked by a never before seen creature. Your character loses his gun and needs to escape. However, your character’s tracking and hunting knowledge gives him the ability to be aware of his scent and mask it temporarily using mud. The more he exerts himself, the stronger his scent will become until the wolf is onto him immediately, so before his scent becomes too strong, he needs to find a way to dampen his scent, which will wear off eventually. He is also skilled at hiding, but it will only work if his scent is weak enough. Your goal is to navigate around the forest, finding items to your advantage and diverting the creatures’s attention away from you.

Stages of Development

Beginning

In the beginning, we worked on the level, adding trees and rivers to create forest scene. We also implemented the first-person controller and imported character models for the hunter and werewolf. Additionally, we added a hiding functionality for the character.

Midpoint

In the midpoint of our project, we worked on animations for the werewolf and implementing the enemy AI which included the patrol/wander, chase, and attack states. We also decided to change up the scene to have bigger trees for our forest. Additionally, we implemented distraction mechanic where the player can throw a meat bone or a rock to divert the werewolf's attention

Final

Towards the end of the semester, we added in a gun which includes a crosshair and animations. We also worked on set design, adding high textured water, flowing grass, and some skeletons with jump scare sounds. We finalized the game with the menu, game over, and win screens.
